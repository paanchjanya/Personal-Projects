Introduction

What is 3D Lung Reconstruction?

3D lung reconstruction is the process of creating three-dimensional models from 2D medical images (CT scans, MRI) to provide better visualization and analysis of lung structures.

Why is it Important?

  • Enhanced diagnostic accuracy
  • Better surgical planning
  • Improved patient education
  • Advanced research capabilities
  • Treatment monitoring

Preprocessing Algorithms:

Gaussian Filter: G(x,y) = (1/2πσ²) × e^(-(x²+y²)/2σ²)

Median Filter: Removes salt-and-pepper noise

Anisotropic Diffusion: Edge-preserving smoothing

3D Reconstruction Methods

Volume Rendering Techniques:

  • Marching Cubes: Isosurface extraction
  • Ray Casting: Direct volume rendering
  • Splatting: Point-based rendering
  • Texture Mapping: GPU-accelerated

Marching Cubes Algorithm:

1. Divide volume into cubes

2. Classify vertices (inside/outside)

3. Generate triangular mesh

4. Smooth surface normals
